In the dawn of creation, before time took flight,
There lay Pangaea, a marvel of light.
Fashioned by the Creator, with mountains so grand,
One mighty expanse, a divine, woven strand.

Yet as the ages passed, by unseen design,
Pangaea shattered, each piece would resign.
Now scattered as nations, on waters they glide,
Fragments of history, where once they did bide.

What is this relic, a testament to fate,
Once whole in its glory, now separated by fate?

A secret lies within, each line holds a key,
Letters in order reveal what’s meant to be.
